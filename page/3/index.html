PTP and timestamping methods

Computers in networks traditionally don’t have any knowledge of when other computers can/will transmit data. Dozens of devices transmitting data whenever they want will inevitably lead to collisions and congestions at one point or another, which in turn leads to packets not arriving as soon as they should. Packets arriving later than intended may affect what the receiver should do with it. Data can expire. If the timeframe for its usefulness has passed then the receiver must know this, or else it may perform actions based on old information....
